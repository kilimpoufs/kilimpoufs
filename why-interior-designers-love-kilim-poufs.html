FAQS

What are Kilim poufs made from?

Kilim poufs are typically made from traditional woven fabrics, specifically kilim textiles, which are created using a flatweave technique. They often feature a blend of wool and cotton, providing durability and comfort.

How can I incorporate Kilim poufs into my interior design?

Kilim poufs can be used in various ways, such as extra seating, footrests, or decorative elements. They can complement both traditional and modern decor styles, making them versatile additions to your space.

Are Kilim poufs suitable for outdoor use?

While Kilim poufs are primarily designed for indoor use, some can be made with water-resistant materials. If using outdoors, it's best to check the manufacturer's specifications or choose poufs specifically designed for outdoor conditions.

Do Kilim poufs require special care?

Kilim poufs should generally be spot cleaned with a damp cloth and mild detergent. It's advisable to avoid excessive moisture and direct sunlight to maintain their colors and texture. Regular vacuuming can help keep them clean.

What makes Kilim poufs a sustainable choice for interior design?

Kilim poufs are often handcrafted using traditional techniques, which can reduce environmental impact. Additionally, many are made from natural fibers, making them a more sustainable choice compared to synthetic alternatives.
